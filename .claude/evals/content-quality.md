# Content Quality Evaluation

Rubric for evaluating the substance and effectiveness of Sharp End Marketing content.

## Criteria

### 1. Substance Density (1-5)

Percentage of content that contains specific, verifiable claims or evidence.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | >80% of content is specific claims, data, or evidence |
| 4 | 60-80% substance; remaining content serves structural purpose |
| 3 | 40-60% substance; noticeable filler or vague statements |
| 2 | 20-40% substance; dominated by generic assertions |
| 1 | <20% substance; mostly platitudes and marketing fluff |

**Substance examples:**
- "40% increase in social media engagement within 3 months" (specific, verifiable)
- "Built a complete brand identity from logo to social templates" (concrete deliverable)
- "30% increase in program enrollments through targeted digital campaigns" (quantified claim)

**Non-substance examples:**
- "We help businesses succeed" (vague)
- "A holistic solution for your marketing" (banned word + generic)
- "Take your brand to the next level" (cliche)

### 2. Assertion Titles (1-5)

All headings make claims or assertions, not labels.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Every heading makes a clear claim or assertion |
| 4 | 1 heading is a label rather than an assertion |
| 3 | Mix of assertions and labels |
| 2 | Most headings are labels ("Features," "Benefits," "About") |
| 1 | All headings are generic labels |

**Good:** "Strategy That Drives Real Results" (claim)
**Bad:** "Our Services" (label)

**Good:** "Why Boutique Agencies Outperform Big Firms" (assertion)
**Bad:** "About Our Agency" (label)

### 3. Narrative Flow (1-5)

Logical progression and clear transitions between sections.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Clear narrative arc: problem, evidence, solution, proof, action |
| 4 | Strong flow with one awkward transition |
| 3 | Sections make sense individually but don't build on each other |
| 2 | Disjointed — sections could be rearranged without impact |
| 1 | No discernible narrative structure |

### 4. Proof Points (1-5)

Every claim has supporting evidence.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Every claim backed by metric, case study, or client example |
| 4 | One claim lacks direct evidence but is reasonable |
| 3 | Several claims unsupported; evidence present but not matched to claims |
| 2 | Most claims are assertions without evidence |
| 1 | No evidence provided for any claim |

**Types of proof:**
- Quantified metrics (with source when possible)
- Named client references or case studies
- Specific deliverables and outcomes
- Before/after comparisons

### 5. Audience Fit (1-5)

Content matches the target persona's language, concerns, and expertise level.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | Language, examples, and concerns perfectly match persona |
| 4 | Strong fit with one section that could better address persona pain points |
| 3 | Generally appropriate but occasionally too technical or too simple |
| 2 | Unclear who the content is written for |
| 1 | Complete mismatch between content and target audience |

### 6. CTA Effectiveness (1-5)

Calls to action are clear, actionable, and appropriate for funnel stage.

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 5 | CTAs are specific, action-oriented, and match the page's funnel stage |
| 4 | CTAs present and clear, one could be more specific |
| 3 | CTAs exist but are generic ("Learn More," "Contact Us") |
| 2 | CTA present but mismatched to funnel stage or content |
| 1 | No clear CTA or CTA is buried/unclear |

**Funnel-appropriate CTAs:**
- Awareness: "See how we helped SmartyPantz build their brand" (case study link)
- Consideration: "Request a Free Marketing Audit" (specific offer)
- Decision: "Get in Touch — Let's Talk Strategy" (personal, low-pressure)

## Pass/Fail Threshold

- **Pass:** Average score >= 4.0 AND substance density >= 4
- **Fail:** Average < 4.0 OR substance density < 4
